## Code of Conduct

Kurir is a small project. We want it to stay welcoming, useful, and pleasant to work on. The rules are short.

### Be kind

Treat everyone with respect. Assume good intent. Disagree with ideas, not people. If a conversation gets heated, step away and come back later.

### Be useful

When you open an issue, explain the problem. When you submit a PR, explain the change. When you review code, explain the suggestion. Vague feedback is not helpful — concrete, kind, specific feedback is.

### What's not OK

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning language
- Harassment of any kind, in issues, PRs, commits, or any other project space
- Discrimination based on who someone is (background, identity, beliefs, appearance, etc.)
- Sharing other people's private information without their consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews

### Scope

This applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, commits, discussions, and any other channel where people interact about Kurir. It also applies when representing the project in public.

### Reporting

If you see behavior that crosses these lines, or you're on the receiving end of it, contact the maintainer privately at **carl-fredrik@arvidson.io**. Reports stay confidential. You won't be retaliated against for reporting in good faith.

### Enforcement

The maintainer will review reports and respond proportionally — anything from a private nudge to a permanent ban from the project. Decisions are final but you can ask for clarification.

---

This is a shortened, project-specific code of conduct. For a more formal alternative, see the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/).
